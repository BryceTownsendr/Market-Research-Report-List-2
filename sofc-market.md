<p><p>Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFCs) are a type of fuel cell that operate at high temperatures. The SOFC market can be categorized into three types: planar, tubular, and others. Planar SOFCs consist of flat layers stacked together, offering high power density and compact design. Tubular SOFCs, on the other hand, have a cylindrical shape with a central electrolyte tube, offering better sealing capabilities and long lifespans. The "others" category includes different cell geometries such as co-fired, flat-tube, and monolithic, each offering unique advantages and application suitability.</p></p>
