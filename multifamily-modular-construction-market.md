<p><p>Multifamily modular construction refers to the process of building multiple housing units or apartment complexes using prefabricated modules. </p><p>Permanent modular construction involves the assembly of modules on-site, creating a permanent structure that cannot be easily relocated. This type of modular construction is ideal for long-term housing needs and is comparable to traditional construction in terms of quality and durability.</p><p>Relocatable modular construction, on the other hand, involves the use of prefabricated modules that can be easily transported and assembled at different locations. These structures are not designed to be permanently fixed and can be moved and reconfigured as needed. Relocatable modular construction offers flexibility and is commonly used for temporary housing, construction site offices, and disaster relief efforts.</p></p>
