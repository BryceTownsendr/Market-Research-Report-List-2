<p><p>The paralleling reactor market includes two main types: oil-immersed reactors and air-core reactors. Oil-immersed reactors are filled with insulating oil, which helps to dissipate heat and improve thermal stability. These reactors are commonly used in high-power applications such as electric power systems and industrial processes. On the other hand, air-core reactors do not contain any insulating oil and rely on air as the cooling medium. They are often used in medium to low-power applications like electrical distribution systems. Both types serve the purpose of maintaining voltage stability and reducing reactive power in electrical systems.</p></p>
