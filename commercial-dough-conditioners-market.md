<p><p>Commercial dough conditioners are food additives used in the baking industry to improve the quality, texture, and shelf life of dough. There are two types of dough conditioners available in the market - powders and fluids. Powdered dough conditioners are dry mixtures typically containing enzymes, emulsifiers, and other ingredients that can be easily blended into dough. On the other hand, fluid dough conditioners are liquid-based additives that serve the same purpose as powders but are in a liquid form. Both types of conditioners provide bakers with options depending on their specific needs and preferences.</p></p>
